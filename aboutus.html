SOME GUIDE FOR OUR CLIENT

some tips for our client of every product

  • AIR CONDITION

    ⦿ AC Not Turning On:
    ● Check the Circuit Breaker: Reset the circuit breaker if it has tripped.
    ● Inspect the Wiring: Look for any visible signs of damage to the wiring.
    ● Replace Batteries: If you have a battery-operated thermostat, try replacing the batteries.
    ⦿ Poor Airflow:
    ●Clean or Replace Filters: Dirty filters can block airflow. Clean or replace them as needed.
    ●Check Vents: Ensure all vents are open and not obstructed.
    ●Clean Coils: Dirty coils can also impede airflow. Carefully clean the evaporator and condenser coils.

  • WASHING MACHINE

    ⦿ Turn Off the Machine
    ● Immediately unplug the washing machine or turn off the circuit breaker to prevent further damage or electrical hazards.
    ⦿Assess the Damage
    ●Check for visible signs of damage such as leaks, broken parts, or burnt smells.
    ⦿Consult the Manual
    ●Refer to the user manual for troubleshooting tips specific to your model.
    ⦿ Professional Help
    If you’re unable to identify or fix the issue, contact a professional appliance repair service. It’s often safer and more effective than attempting complex repairs yourself.

  • HOME/COMMERCIAL REFRIGERATOR

    ⦿Check the Power Supply:
    ●Ensure the refrigerator is plugged in properly.
    ●Check if the outlet is working by plugging in another appliance.
    ●Verify that the circuit breaker hasn't tripped.
    ⦿ Inspect the Thermostat:
    ●Make sure the thermostat is set to the correct temperature.
    ●Adjust the temperature settings and see if it helps.
    ⦿ Check the Door Seals:
    ●Inspect the door gaskets for cracks or tears.
    ●Clean the seals with warm, soapy water to ensure a tight seal.
    ●Replace damaged gaskets if necessary.
    ⦿ Look for Leaks:
    ●Check for any signs of coolant leaks.
    ●Leaking coolant requires professional repair.

  • WATER PURIFIER

    ⦿Turn Off the Water Supply: If you suspect a leak or other major damage, turn off the water supply to the purifier to prevent flooding or further damage.
    ⦿Unplug the Unit: If your water purifier is electrically powered, unplug it to avoid any risk of electric shock.
    ⦿Check for Visible Damage: Inspect the unit for any visible signs of damage, such as cracks in the housing, broken hoses, or loose connections.

  • TV AND HOME THEATER

    ⦿ Check Power Supply:
    ●Ensure that the power cable is securely connected.
    ●Check if the outlet is working by plugging in another device.
    ●Try a different power cable if possible.
    ⦿ Inspect for Physical Damage:
    ●Look for any visible signs of damage on the device, such as cracks, dents, or exposed Wires
    ●Check the cables for any signs of wear or fraying.
    ⦿ Restart the Device:
    ●Turn off the device and unplug it for a few minutes before plugging it back in and turning it on again.

  • INVETOR AND BATTERY

    ⦿ Identify the Issue:
    ●Check Display/Error Codes: Many modern inverters have displays or indicators that show error codes. Refer to the manual to understand the issue.
    ●Physical Inspection: Look for visible damage, burnt components, or loose connections.
    ●Reset the Inverter
    ⦿ for battery
    ●Check Voltage: Use a multimeter to check the battery voltage.
    ●Inspect for Leaks or Swelling: Look for any physical signs of damage such as leaks, bulging, or swelling.
